### Supervised Machine Learning to Predict Student Proficiency in Oregon Public Schools  
**Author:** Bhavjot Khurana  
**Institution:** Reed College  
**Degree Awarded:** Bachelor of Arts in Mathematics, May 2023  
**Advisor:** Prof. Njesa Totty  

**Abstract:**  
In this thesis, I built and evaluated supervised learning models to predict student proficiency scores in English Language Arts and Mathematics across Oregon public schools. The models—decision trees and random forests—were trained on data from the Oregon Department of Education (2018–19) and included features such as faculty resources, poverty rating, student participation, and demographic group. To address class imbalance and ensure fairness across subgroups, I employed stratified sampling and SMOTE. Key techniques involved cross-validation, hyperparameter tuning via the `caret` package, and variable importance metrics. My findings suggest that while decision trees provide interpretability, random forests deliver superior predictive performance. This work has implications for educational policy, accountability, and resource allocation.

**Skills Demonstrated:**  
- Data wrangling and preprocessing in R  
- Supervised machine learning (random forest, decision trees)  
- Model evaluation with confusion matrices and F1-scores  
- Data visualization using `ggplot2` and `rpart.plot`  
- Educational data analysis under ESSA/NCLB policy frameworks
