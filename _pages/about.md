I’m a **data enthusiast with a background in statistics, machine learning, and education**, focused on using product data to understand how people learn, engage, and make decisions. My work spans predictive modeling, experimentation, and data infrastructure—everything needed to move from raw events to meaningful change in how we build tools and experiences.

For the past 15 months, I’ve also worked as a math and stats tutor, supporting a broad range of students across different levels and learning styles. That hands-on experience has fueled my curiosity about the intersection of education and data: how we can better design systems that adapt to learners, not the other way around.

Whether it’s tracking student progress with rubric-based models, building simulations for learning behavior, or designing *qscroll*, a test-prep platform built around providing students with important data and rapid feedback loops. I care about collecting the right data and designing systems that evolve with it. I’m also exploring how to integrate large language models into real workflows from tutoring interfaces to dataset expansion to create more adaptive, intelligent tools.

You can explore some of my work on GitHub, or reach out if you’re working on a product where data is central to the mission. 

If you're curious, check out my projects page and blog. I write about what I’m building, what I’m learning, and my general interests, especially the recent *Powerlifitng America Nationals*, I am so exicted about the World Games!
