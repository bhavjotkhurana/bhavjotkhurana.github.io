I’m a **data enthusiast** with a background in **statistics**, **machine learning**, and **education**, focused on using **product data** to understand how people learn, engage, and make decisions. I care about building systems that go beyond surface-level metrics to capture something *real* about how people interact with learning tools.

- **I work across predictive modeling, experimentation, and data infrastructure**, turning raw usage data into decisions that improve tools and experiences. I’m especially interested in the interplay between *structure and behavior*—how data systems can shape better learning environments.

- For the past **15 months**, I’ve been a **math and stats tutor**, working with students from *high school to college* across a range of learning needs. That hands-on experience drives my interest in the **intersection of education and data**—how we can design systems that *adapt to learners*, not the other way around.

- I'm building projects like **_qScroll_**, a minimalist, scroll-based SAT prep app with built-in feedback loops. I’ve also worked on **rubric-based models** to measure instructional impact and created **simulations** to better understand how students engage with content.

- I’m actively exploring **AI (LLM) integration** in educational tools—building **intelligent tutoring interfaces**, **adaptive feedback systems**, and **automated dataset expansion** to personalize learning at scale.

You can explore my work on [**GitHub**](https://github.com/bhavjotkhurana), or get in touch if you’re working on a product where **data isn’t just a byproduct—it’s central to how the thing works**.  Also, check out my [**projects page**](https://bhavjotkhurana.github.io/portfolio/) and [**blog**](https://bhavjotkhurana.github.io/year-archive/) to see what I’m building, thinking through, and writing about.  Most recently, I covered the **2025 Powerlifting America Nationals** and am super excited about the **World Championships in Germany**!
