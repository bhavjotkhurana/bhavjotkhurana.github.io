I’m a data enthusiast working at the intersection of **education, AI, and behavioral data**, with hands-on experience in **Python, R, SQL**, and the **OpenAI API**. I build tools and systems that don’t just track what people do, but try to understand *why* they do it.

- My recent work focuses on **integrating LLMs into educational products**, building things like **adaptive tutoring interfaces**, **automated dataset pipelines**, and **feedback loops** that actually respond to how learners interact.

- I work across **predictive modeling**, **experimentation**, and **data infrastructure**, turning messy usage data into insights that help improve how people learn, engage, and make decisions. You can see this in action in projects like [**_qScroll_**](https://bhavjotkhurana.github.io/portfolio/): a scroll-based SAT prep app with real-time feedback built into its core.

- I’ve spent the past **15 months as a math and stats tutor**, working with students from high school to college. That experience grounds everything I build. It’s where I learned how variable learning really is, and why personalization isn’t just a buzzword.

- Lately, I’ve been diving into **cybersecurity and systems design**, learning how **data centers work**, how machines talk to each other, and why low-level knowledge matters. (I'm currently taking courses through [Low Level Academy](https://lowlevel.academy/courses).)

You can check out my [**GitHub**](https://github.com/bhavjotkhurana), [**projects**](https://bhavjotkhurana.github.io/portfolio/), or [**blog**](https://bhavjotkhurana.github.io/year-archive/) to see what I’m building, thinking through, and writing about. Most recently, I covered the **2025 Powerlifting Nationals**—and yes, I'm counting down the days to the **World Championships in Germany**!
