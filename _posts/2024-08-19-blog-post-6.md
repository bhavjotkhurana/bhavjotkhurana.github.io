As AI use increases and I see my usage also go up in my tutoring and data work, I started noticing how it remembers things I have said in previous sessions. I would mention a project one day, then see the model refer to it later without prompting. That was when I learned about something called Model Context.

Model Context lets the model store selected information across sessions. This includes things like my name, the tools I use, what I tutor, and current projects. It does not seem to remember everything - just what deems "useful" or I can ask it to store something specific (like I don't enjoy emoji use).

I get why it exists. It saves time and adds continuity. When it works well, I do not have to repeat myself, and the model’s suggestions feel more aligned with how I think. It is useful when I am troubleshooting something technical or juggling multiple tasks.

Still, I hesitate. The control is there and I can see what it remembers, and I can reset it, but now I have to manage that memory like another tool. If something changes in how I work, I need to update the model or it will keep leaning on outdated context. Also, the idea of an AI model having this much context on you has the dystopian hint to it. 

There are times when that context is more limiting than helpful. The model might assume something still applies when it does not, or bring up something I have already moved past. It sometimes feels like it is referring to a fixed version of me instead of staying flexible. That said, I think it is an interesting shift. Model Context is trying to solve some problems: how to make these tools work over time, not just in isolated prompts. At the same time, a data leak would mean losing personal information beyond my Crumbl Cookies password.
